# One feature I'd add that most people won't think of

At the very end, add a page called:

> **What Changed My Mind**

For every marketing concept, document:

* My original assumption
* What the research showed
* What I changed
* Why I changed it

For example:

| Concept   | Initial Assumption | Research Result                                        | Decision                          |
| --------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| Pricing   | ₹10,000            | Customers associate low prices with generic quality    | Increased target price to ₹14,500 |
| Customer  | College students   | Young professionals also fit the emotional positioning | Expanded target audience          |
| Messaging | Event planning     | Memory creation resonated more strongly                | Shifted brand messaging           |

This shows **decision-making**, not just knowledge. Recruiters and hiring managers care much more about *how you think* than whether you can recite marketing frameworks.
